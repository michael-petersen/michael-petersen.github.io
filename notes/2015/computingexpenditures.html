How much computing budget do I need?

(This post is backdated to when I switched to my current setup, but it features more recent updates: most recently 26 May 2022.)


I fully admit that I'm a bit weird about spending grant money and new computers. I've personally purchased all of the laptops that I use for science research (although I'm a huge proponent of shared resources, and computing clusters), and I use the computers as workhorses for absolutely everything I want to do with a computer. I fully admit that I waited too long to upgrade my first graduate school laptop, which I write more about below. It is also possible I waited to long to upgrade my first postdoc laptop.


To that end, I try to both (a) keep compulsive track of how much I'm spending, and (b) try to understand as much as possible why I am investing in something. I'm also counting all my computer peripherals.

Postdoc II (2022-)


I'm currently on a MacBook Air (M1, 2020). I ordered this machine on May 19th, 2022, from Apple.com, for $1,509. I also attempted to future-proof this machine, buy purchasing the Apple M1 chip with 8‑core CPU, 8‑core GPU, and 16‑core Neural Engine, 16GB unified memory, and 1TB SSD. Mac watchers will say this was a bad time to upgrade, as a new line is expected in Fall 2022. I am less convinced, but time will tell.

Grad School II/Postdoc I (2015-2022)


From May 2015 to May 2022, I was on a MacBook Pro (Retina, 13-inch, Early 2015). I ordered this machine on April 27th, 2015. I purchased this machine from Apple.com, for $1,878. Crucially, I chose to try and 'future-proof' this machine by upgrading to the 3.1GHz Dual-core Intel Core i7, Turbo Boost up to 3.4GHz, with 16GB 1866MHz LPDDR3 SDRAM, and 256GB PCIe-based Flash Storage. In the end, the 256GB storage turns out to be the bottleneck that I've had to wrestle with.

Grad School I (2010-2015)


From June 2010 to May 2015, I was on a MacBook Pro (13-inch, Early 2010). I purchased this machine off the shelf for $1,049, in person, in MA. As this machine was part of a generation where computers could be more easily modified, I upgraded this computer myself in two stages, to prolong its life. In retrospect, I should have gotten a more powerful processor to start. It is still currently working as a backup! However, the OS has reached the absolute maximum that will ever be supported (10.13), so the days are relatively numbered. As of 2022, homebrew will no longer support 10.13.6, so failures are starting to pop up in compiler-related projects.
